THE FOUNDATION Guiding Obligations

The 7 Principles

Principles are the bedrock of PRINCE2. They are universal, self-validating, and empowering. If any of these are not applied, it is not a PRINCE2 project.

Overview Principles Practices Processes Documents Glossary
01

Continued Business Justification

A PRINCE2 project requires a justifiable reason to start it, and that justification must remain valid throughout its life. It is documented in the Business Case.

  • Prevents projects from continuing if they no longer offer value.
  • Justification must be aligned with the Business Objectives.

Exam Key Point

If the project is no longer justifiable, the Project Manager must escalate this to the Project Board. The Board has the authority to stop the project early.

02

Learn from Experience

PRINCE2 teams should actively seek lessons from previous projects. These are captured in the Lessons Log and passed on to future projects.

Everyone is responsible for learning: the Board, the PM, and the Team.

Timeline of Learning

  • Start: Research previous lessons.
  • During: Record new lessons in the Lessons Log.
  • Close: Include lessons in the End Project Report.
03

Defined Roles and Responsibilities

A project must have an agreed team structure that includes the interests of Business, Users, and Suppliers.

Executive Senior User Senior Supplier

The Project Board

The "Executive" is ultimately accountable for the project's success, supported by the Users (who need the products) and Suppliers (who build them).

04

Manage by Stages

PRINCE2 mandates at least two management stages: the Initiation Stage and at least one Delivery Stage.

Breaking the project into stages allows for better control and "go/no-go" decision points.

Management vs Technical

PRINCE2 focuses on Management Stages (decision points) rather than Technical Stages (type of work like 'Design' or 'Build').

05

Manage by Exception

Authority is delegated by setting Tolerances. The higher level only gets involved if these tolerances are predicted to be exceeded.

06

Focus on Products

Don't start with "What are we doing?" (Activities). Start with "What are we building?" (Products).

Every product must have a Product Description to ensure quality expectations are clear.

"Focus on the output, not just the work. If you don't know what you are building, you cannot plan for it."
07

Tailor to Suit the Project

PRINCE2 is not a rigid "one size fits all" method. It must be adapted to suit the projectâ€™s size, complexity, importance, capability, and risk.

The Tailoring Rule

Tailoring is mandatory. You don't skip parts of PRINCE2; you scale them down or up to fit the context.
