The 7 Principles

Principles are the bedrock of PRINCE2. They are universal, self-validating, and empowering. If any are missing, it is not a PRINCE2 project.

Overview Principles Practices Processes Documents Glossary
01

Continued Business Justification

A project requires a justifiable reason to start, and that reason must remain valid throughout its life. This is documented in the Business Case.

  • Aligns project costs and risks against expected benefits.

Decision Point

If justification is lost, the Project Manager must escalate to the Board for early closure.

02

Learn from Experience

PRINCE2 teams actively seek and record lessons. Don't reinvent the wheel; use the Lessons Log to drive improvement.

Timeline

Start: Review past logs.
During: Record new findings.
Close: Report in the End Project Report.
03

Defined Roles & Responsibilities

A project must represent the interests of the Business, Users, and Suppliers.

EXECUTIVE (Business)The single individual accountable for the Business Case.
SENIOR USERSpecifies needs and uses products to realize benefits.
SENIOR SUPPLIERProvides resources and technical expertise.

"The Project Manager is NOT on the Project Board. They report TO it."

04

Manage by Stages

Projects are planned and controlled one stage at a time. Every project must have at least two stages: Initiation and at least one delivery stage.

Management stages are decision points for the Board to decide if the project should continue.

05

Manage by Exception

Board members only intervene when Tolerances are forecast to be exceeded.

"This principle saves the Board's time while maintaining control."

06

Focus on Products

PRINCE2 focuses on the definition and delivery of products. Every product needs a Product Description to ensure quality expectations are met.

"If you don't know what you are building, you cannot accurately plan for it."
07

Tailor to Suit

PRINCE2 is not rigid. It must be adapted to the project's size, environment, complexity, and risk.

Mandatory

Tailoring is not skipping parts of the method; it is scaling them to be appropriate for the context.
