OUTPUTS PRINCE2 7 Management Products

Management Products

PRINCE2 identifies 26 management products. For the Foundation exam, you must distinguish between Baselines (plans), Records (dynamic logs), and Reports (status updates).

Overview Principles Practices Processes Roles Documents Glossary

The PID

Project Initiation Documentation

The PID is a logical "container" that gathers key information required to manage the project. It is assembled during the Initiating a Project (IP) process.

Approach: Sustainability

Defines how environmental and social targets will be managed (New in V7).

Approach: Communication

Specifies who needs what information, when, and in what format.

Approach: Change & Issue

Procedures for handling change requests and project issues.

Approach: Risk

Methods for identifying, assessing, and responding to risks.

Approach: Data & Digital

Use of digital tools and data management (New in V7).

Project Plan

The high-level schedule, resource requirements, and control points.

Baseline Products

Project Brief

SU Process

Used to provide a firm foundation for initiation. Contains the Outline Business Case.

Work Package

CS Process

The "contract" between the PM and Team Manager defining the work to be done.

Benefits Plan

V7 Focus

Defines how and when project benefits will be measured and realized.

Records & Logs

Project Log

In Version 7, this is the repository for the Daily Log, Lessons Log, Issue, and Risk Registers.

Quality Register

Records all planned and completed quality activities (tests, inspections).

Product Status Account

A snapshot report on the status of products at any given time.

Report Products

Highlight Report

PM to Board

Periodic report on stage progress and future outlook.

Checkpoint Report

TM to PM

Status report on the execution of a specific Work Package.

End Stage Report

SB Process

Summary of the stage for the Board to decide whether to continue.
