Management Products

PRINCE2 identifies 26 management products. Below are the most critical ones for the Foundation exam, categorized by their type.

Overview Principles Practices Processes Documents Glossary

Baseline Products

Project Brief

Process: Starting Up (SU)

Used to provide a full and firm foundation for the initiation of the project. Contains the Outline Business Case.

PID (Project Initiation Documentation)

Process: Initiating a Project (IP)

The single source of truth. It defines the project's 'why, what, when, and how'. It is the Contract between the PM and the Board.

Work Package

Process: Controlling a Stage (CS)

The 'contract' between the PM and the Team Manager. Defines the work to be done and its constraints.

Records & Logs

Daily Log

Used by the PM to record informal notes and issues that do not need a formal entry in the Issue Register.

Lessons Log

A repository for lessons learned during the project or from previous projects. Crucial for the Learn from Experience principle.

Issue Register

Used to capture and maintain information on all of the issues that are being formally managed.

Report Products

Highlight Report

Frequency: Periodic

From PM to Project Board. Provides a summary of the stage status at intervals defined in the PID.

Checkpoint Report

Frequency: Periodic

From Team Manager to PM. Provides a report of progress against a Work Package.

Exception Report

Triggered by: Exception

Produced by the PM when a stage or project is forecast to exceed tolerance levels.

Baseline, Record, or Report? Know the difference for the exam.
